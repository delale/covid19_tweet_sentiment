Time Series¶

This week you have learned the fundamentals of time series. The goal of this exercise is to make useful visualizations on a time series dataset.

Time Series is a sequence of observations recorded at different time periods Time series may depend on different frequencies such as hours, weeks, months and years. Certain examples of time series are:

  • Stock prices per day
  • Production of a product
  • Global Daily Temperature

After this session you will be able to:

  • observe how time affects a variable
  • inspect periodic fluctuations (seasonality)
  • evaluate the variable alterations year (trend) and monthly wise (different way of seasonality visualization)

In the next session we will dive deeper in time series with stationarity testing, detrending techniques, ARIMA models and more.

4. Seasonal plot¶

To investigate if there are fluctuations we need inspect the seasonality of our time-series.

  • First we extract the month and the year from our date column.
  • Then we use the seaborn built in function lineplot to get a curve for each year.

The difference with the previous figure is that the data are plotted agains the individual "seasons". The data for each season are overlapped.

Is there any fluctuation you observe over the years?

# Prepare data
